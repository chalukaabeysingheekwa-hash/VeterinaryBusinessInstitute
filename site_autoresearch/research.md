Date of research: March 30, 2026

## Site-wide findings

1. The live VBI site has real educational content, but the information architecture is weak.
2. The Dominatelaw reference works because it repeats a clear promise, shows routes early, and keeps offering the next step.
3. VBI had outdated event framing on the homepage. On March 30, 2026 the homepage still highlighted a webinar path tied to February 19, 2026 language.
4. Several VBI pages were thin or generic, especially marketing and contact. The redesign needed stronger hierarchy rather than only better styling.
5. The Vimeo folder added an overlooked asset: 12 event panels across leadership, culture, compliance, technology, wellbeing, client communication, and financial health.

## Page-by-page upgrade targets

### Home

- Replace generic educational copy with a more direct value proposition.
- Use a real featured episode instead of placeholder "coming soon" style cards.
- Treat webinar content as replay-ready archive material, not only event promotion.
- Route visitors clearly into About, Podcast, Webinars, Marketing, and Contact.

### About

- Keep the core mission from the live About page, but say it faster.
- Explain why VBI exists as its own platform rather than leaving it as a light brand wrapper.
- Turn the ending into a next-step section instead of a simple sign-off.

### Podcast

- Move from a raw archive list into editorial browsing.
- Surface host authority and episode summaries.
- Use topic-based framing so visitors understand what the archive covers.

### Webinars

- Fix the stale-event problem by framing the page around replays.
- Add dates, summaries, and clear reasons to watch each session.
- Use archive language that still works after the event date passes.

### Events

- Convert the Vimeo review folder into a first-class route instead of leaving it off-site and undiscoverable.
- Use dates, categories, durations, and summaries to make the panel series browseable.
- Link panels back into webinars, podcast content, and marketing support so the platform feels unified.

### Marketing

- Expand beyond a short sales block into a real audit explanation.
- Use the six-factor review, local market analysis, and proof points from the live page.
- Make the CTA feel like the natural outcome of the explanation.

### Contact

- Turn a plain info block into a page with clear reasons to reach out.
- Support softer entry points for users who are not ready to book an audit.
- Keep contact connected to the broader content hub with route links and social follow-up.

## Fixed evaluation philosophy

The evaluator checks for:

- Metadata per route
- Minimum section depth
- CTA coverage
- Asset usage
- Expected keyword coverage
- Internal route linking

That is intentionally simple. It is not trying to judge taste. It is enforcing structure so future iterations do not drift back toward thin pages.
